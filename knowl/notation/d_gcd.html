Definition 1.18. Greatest common divisor.

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be integers, one of which is nonzero. The greatest common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) denoted \(\gcd(a,b)\text{,}\) is the greatest positive integer dividing \(a\) and \(b\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \gcd(a,b)=\max \{n\in \Z_{> 0}\mid n\mid a \text{ and } n\mid b\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
