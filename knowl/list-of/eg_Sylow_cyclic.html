Example 1.161. Sylow subgroups of cyclic groups.

Let \(G=\angvec{g}\) be a finite cyclic group of cardinality \(n=\ord g\text{.}\) Fix a prime divisor \(p\) of \(n\text{,}\) and let \(v_p(n)=k\text{,}\) so that \(n=p^km\text{,}\) where \(p\nmid m\text{.}\) From TheoremÂ 1.82, we know there is exactly one subgroup of \(G\) of cardinality \(p^k\text{:}\) namely, \(H=\angvec{g^{n/p^k}}=\angvec{g^m}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \Syl_p(G)=\{\angvec{g^m}\} \end{equation*}
and \(n_p(G)=1\text{.}\)
