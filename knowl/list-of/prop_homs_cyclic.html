Proposition 1.191. Homomorphisms from cyclic groups.

Let \(G=\angvec{g}\) be a cyclic group, and let \(H\) be a group.
  1. If \(\ord g=\infty\text{,}\) then the map
    \begin{align*} \Hom(G,H) \amp \rightarrow H\\ \phi \amp \mapsto \phi(g) \end{align*}
    gives a bijection between \(\Hom(G,H)\) and elements of \(H\text{.}\) In more detail, for all \(\phi\in \Hom(G,H)\text{,}\) there is a unique \(h\in H\) such that
    \begin{equation*} \phi(g^i)=h^i \end{equation*}
    for all \(i\in \Z\text{.}\)
