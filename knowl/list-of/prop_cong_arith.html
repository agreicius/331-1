Proposition 1.22. Modular arithmetic.

Fix a modulus \(n\text{.}\) Assume integers \(a,a',b,b'\) satisfy
\begin{align*} a \amp \equiv a' \pmod n \amp b\amp \equiv b'\pmod n\text{.} \end{align*}
We have
\begin{align} a+b \amp \equiv a'+b'\pmod n \amp \tag{1.9}\\ a b\amp \equiv a' b' \pmod n \amp \text{.}\tag{1.10} \end{align}
It follows from this that
\begin{align} [a+b]_n \amp = [a'+b']_n \amp \tag{1.11}\\ [ab]_n\amp = [a'b']_n \text{.}\tag{1.12} \end{align}
