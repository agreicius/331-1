Proof.

  1. This is a straightforward proof by induction, the base case \(k=2\) being trivial. For the induction step, assume any \(k\)-cycle can be written as in (1.16.1). Given any \((k+1)\)-cycle \(\sigma=(a_1\ a_2 \dots \ a_{k+1}\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} \sigma\amp =(a_1\ a_{k+1})(a_1\ a_2\ \dots a_k)\\\\ \amp =(a_1\ a_{k+1})(a_1\ a_{k})\cdots (a_1 a_2) \amp (\text{induc. hypo.}) \text{,} \end{align*}
    as desired.
