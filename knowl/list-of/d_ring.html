Definition 2.1.1. Ring.

A ring is a triple \((R,+,\cdot)\text{,}\) where \(R\) is a nonempty set, and \(+\) and \(\cdot\) are binary operations
\begin{align*} R\times R \amp \xrightarrow{\phantom{XX}+\phantom{XX}} R \amp R\times R\amp \xrightarrow{\phantom{XX}\bullet\phantom{XX}} R\text{,} \end{align*}
called ring addition and ring multiplication, respectively, that satisfy the following axioms.
  1. \((R,+)\) is an abelian group.
