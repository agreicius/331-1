Example 2.51. Ideals of \(M_n(R)\).

Let \(R\) be comutative ring. Prove that all ideals of \(M_n(R)\) are of the form \(M_n(I)\) for some ideal \(I\subseteq R\text{,}\) where we define
\begin{align*} M_n(I) \amp = \{[a_{ij}]\in M_n(R)\mid a_{ij}\in I\}\text{.} \end{align*}
In particular, given a field \(K\text{,}\) the only ideals of \(M_n(K)\) are \(\{0\}\) and \(M_n(K)\) itself.
