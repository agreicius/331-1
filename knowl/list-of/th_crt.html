Theorem 2.97. Chinese remainder theorem (CRT).

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, and let \(I_1, I_2, \dots, I_n\) be ideals of \(R\text{.}\) For all \(1\leq k\leq n\text{,}\) we let \(\pi_k\colon R\rightarrow R/I_k\) be the quotient map, and we define
\begin{align*} \phi\colon R \amp \rightarrow \prod_{k=1}^n R/I_k\\ r \amp \mapsto (\pi_1(r), \pi_2(r),\dots, \pi_n(r)) \text{.} \end{align*}
  1. \(\phi\) is a ring homomorphism with kernel
    \begin{align*} \ker\phi \amp =\bigcap_{k=1}^n I_k\text{.} \end{align*}
