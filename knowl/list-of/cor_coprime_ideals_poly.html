Corollary 2.106. Coprime ideals: monic irreducibles.

Let \(F\) be a field. If \(f\) and \(g\) are distinct monic irreducible polynomials in \(F[x]\text{,}\) then the ideals \((f)\) and \((g)\) are coprime.

Proof.

We use condition (3) of PropositionÂ 2.100. Suppose \(h\in F[x]\) divides \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\) Since \(f\) is irreducible, either \(h(x)=c\in F^*\) is a unit, or \(h=cf\) for some unit \(c\in F^*\text{.}\) If the latter is the case, then since \(h=cf\mid g\) it follows that \(f\mid g\text{.}\) But since \(g\) is irreducible and \(f\) is not a unit, we must have \(f=dg\) for some unit \(d\in F^*\text{.}\) Lastly, since \(f\) and \(g\) are monic, it follows that \(d=1\) and \(f=g\text{:}\) a contradiction. We conclude that \(h\) is a unit, showing that the only common divisors of \(f\) and \(g\) are units. Thus \((f)\) and \((g)\) are relatively prime.
