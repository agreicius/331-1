Corollary 2.80. Quotient of polynomial rings.

Let \(R\) be a nontrivial commutative ring, and let \(f\in R\) be a polynomial of degree \(n> 1\) whose leading coefficient is a unit in \(R\text{.}\) Additionally, let \(\pi\colon R[x]\rightarrow R[x]/(f)\) be the quotient map, and let \(\overline{x}=\pi(x)\text{.}\) More concretely, setting \(I=(f)\text{,}\) we have \(\overline{x}=x+I\in R[x]/I\text{.}\)
  1. The restriction \(\pi\vert_R\colon R\rightarrow R[x]/(f)\) is injective. Thus we can identify \(\pi(R)\) with \(R\text{,}\) and we will write \(\pi(r)=r\) for all \(r\in R\text{.}\)
