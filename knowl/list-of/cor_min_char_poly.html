Corollary 2.21.5.

Let \(V\) be an \(n\)-dimensional \(F\)-vector space, and let \(T\in \End_F(V)\) be a nonzero linear transformation. We treat \(V\) as an \(F[x]\)-module via the action of \(T\) on \(V\text{.}\)
  1. Let \((a_i(x))_{i=1}^m\) be the monic invariant factors of \(V\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{align*} m_T(x) \amp = a_m(x)\\ \ch_T(x) \amp =\prod_{i=1}^m a_i(x)\text{.} \end{align*}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Let \(p_1(x),p_2(x),\dots, p_r(x)\) be the distinct monic irreducible polynomials appearing in the elementary factors decomposition of \(V\text{,}\) and for each \(1\leq i\leq r\text{,}\) let \((n_{ij})_j\) be the accompanying unique sequence of integers. We have
    \begin{align*} m_T(x) \amp = \prod_{i=1}^r p_i(x)^{n_{im_i}} \\ \ch_T(x) \amp = \prod p_{i}(x)^{n_{ij}} \text{.} \end{align*}
