Example 2.69. Maximal ideals of \(\Z\).

Let \(n\) be a nonnegative integer. Prove that the ideal \((n)\) is maximal if and only if \(n=p\) is prime.

Solution.
We have
\begin{align} (n) \text{ maximal} \amp \iff (n)\subseteq (a) \implies (m)=(a) \text{ or } (a)=\Z \tag{2.53}\\ \amp \iff a\mid n \implies a=\pm n \text{ or } a=\pm 1\tag{2.54}\\ \amp \iff n \prime\text{.}\tag{2.55} \end{align}
