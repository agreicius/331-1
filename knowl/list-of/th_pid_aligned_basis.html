Theorem 2.19.1. PID-module theorem: aligned bases.

Let \(R\) be a PID. Let \(M\) be free \(R\)-module of finite rank \(m\text{,}\) and let \(N\) be a nonzero submodule of \(M\text{.}\)
  1. \(N\) is a free \(R\)-module and \(\rank N\leq m\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. There is a basis \(B=(m_i)_{i=1}^m\) of \(M\) and nonzero elements \(a_1,a_2,\dots, a_n\in R\text{,}\) \(n\leq m\text{,}\) such that
    \begin{align} a_i \amp \mid a_{i+1}\tag{2.19.1} \end{align}
    for all \(1\leq i\leq n-1\text{,}\) and
    \begin{align} B' \amp =(a_im_i)_{i=1}^{n}\tag{2.19.2} \end{align}
    is a basis of \(N\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
🔗

Proof.
