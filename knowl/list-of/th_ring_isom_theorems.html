Theorem 2.59. Isomorphism theorems for rings.

Let \(R\) be a ring.
  1. First isomorphism theorem.
    Given a ring homomorphism \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) with kernel \(I=\ker\phi\text{,}\) we have \(R/I\cong im\phi\) via the isomorphism \(\overline{\phi}(r+I)=\phi(r)\text{.}\)

  3. Third isomorphism theorem.
    Let \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) be a surjective ring homomorphism. If \(J\) is an ideal of \(R\) containing \(\ker \phi\text{,}\) then \(\phi(J)\) is an ideal of \(S\text{,}\) and we have
    \begin{align*} R/J \amp \cong S/\phi(J)\text{.} \end{align*}
    In particular, considering the quotient map \(\pi\colon R\rightarrow R/I\text{,}\) we see that if \(I\subseteq J\) is a chain of ideals in \(R\text{,}\) then \(J/I\) is an ideal of \(R/I\text{,}\) and we have
    \begin{align*} (R/I)/(J/I)\amp \cong R/J \end{align*}

  4. Fourth isomorphism theorem.
    Let \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) be a surjective ring homomorphism. The map
    \begin{align*} J \amp \mapsto \phi(J) \end{align*}
    is a bijection between the set of ideals of \(R\) that contain \(\ker\phi\) and the set of ideals of \(S\text{,}\) with inverse given by
    \begin{align*} J'\subseteq S \amp \mapsto \phi^{-1}(J')\text{.} \end{align*}
    Moreover, this bijection preserves just about all ideal operations and relations: e.g., inclusion, intersection, sum, product, etc..
