Example 2.29. Polynomial degree.

Find polynomials \(f,g\in \Z/4\Z[x]\) satisfying \(\deg fg< \deg f+\deg g\text{.}\)

Solution.
Let \(f(x)=2x+1\) and \(g(x)=2x\text{.}\) We have \(\deg f=\deg g=1\) and
\begin{align} \deg fg \amp =\deg(4x^2+2x)\tag{2.20}\\ \amp =\deg(2x)\tag{2.21}\\ \amp = 1\tag{2.22}\\ \amp < 1+1\tag{2.23}\\ \amp =\deg f+\deg g\text{.}\tag{2.24} \end{align}
