Corollary 2.13.7. Sums of two squares.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer with factorization
\begin{align*} n \amp = 2^k\cdot \prod_{j=1}^{r}p_j^{n_j}\cdot \prod_{k=1}^s q_k^{m_k}\text{,} \end{align*}
where the \(p_j\) and \(q_k\) are distinct primes, \(p_j\equiv 1 \pmod 4\) for all \(j\text{,}\) and \(q_k\equiv 3\pmod 4\) for all \(k\text{.}\) We have \(n=a^2+b^2\) for integers \(a\) and \(b\) if and only if \(m_k\) is even for all \(k\text{.}\)
