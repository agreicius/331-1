Corollary 2.102. Euler totient function.

Let \(\varphi\) be the Euler totient function: i.e., \(\varphi(m)\) is the number of integers in \(\{1,2,\dots, m\}\) that are relatively prime to \(m\text{.}\) If \(a_1,a_2,\dots, a_n\) are pairwise relatively prime integers, then
\begin{align} \phi(a_1a_2\cdots a_n) \amp = \phi(a_1)\phi(a_2)\cdots \phi(a_n) \text{.}\tag{2.64} \end{align}
Additionally, given \(m=\prod_{k=1}^r p_k^{n_k}\text{,}\) where the \(p_k\) are distinct prime integers, we have
\begin{align} \varphi(m) \amp = \prod_{k=1}^{r}p^{n_k-1}(p_k-1) \text{.}\tag{2.65} \end{align}
