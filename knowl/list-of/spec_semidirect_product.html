Specimen 16. Semidirect product.

Let \(G_1\) and \(G_2\) be groups, and let \(\phi\colon G_2\rightarrow \Aut(G_1)\) be a group homomorphism. The semidirect product of \(G_1\) and \(G_2\) is the group \(G_1\rtimes_\phi G_2\) whose underlying set is the Cartesian product \(G_1\times G_2\) and whose group operation is defined as
\begin{equation} (g_1,g_2)\cdot (g_1',g_2')=(g_1\phi_{g_2}(g_1'),g_2g_2')\text{,}\tag{1.83} \end{equation}
where \(\phi_{g_2}\) is the automorphism \(\phi(g_2)\in \Aut(G_1)\text{.}\)
