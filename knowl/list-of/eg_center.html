Example 2.1.9. Center of a ring.

Let \(R\) be a ring. Show that \(Z(R)\) is a subring of \(R\text{.}\)

Solution.
First we show that \(Z(R)\) is a subring of \((R,+)\text{.}\) From PropositionÂ 2.1.6 we know that \(0\in Z(R)\text{.}\) Next, assume \(a,b\in Z(R)\text{.}\) Given any \(c\in R\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} (a+b)c \amp =ac+bc=ca+cb\\ \amp =ca+cb\\ \amp = c(a+b)\\ (-a)c \amp =a(-c)\\ \amp =(-c)a\\ \amp =c(-a)\text{,} \end{align*}
and thus \(a+b, -a\in Z(R)\text{.}\)
