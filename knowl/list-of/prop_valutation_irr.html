Proposition 2.12.7. Valuation at irreducible.

Let \(R\) be a unique factorization domain.
  1. Given a nonzero element \(a\in R\) and an irreducible element \(p\in R\text{,}\) there is a unique nonnegative integer \(v_p(a)=n\) such that \(a=p^n\, b\text{,}\) where \(b\in R\) and \(p\nmid b\text{.}\) Equivalently, \(v_p(a)\) is the largest nonnegative integer such that \(p^{v_p(a)}\mid a\text{.}\)
