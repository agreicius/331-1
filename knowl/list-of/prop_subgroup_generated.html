Proposition 1.81. Subgroup generated by a set.

Let \(A\) be a subset of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. \(\angvec{A}\) is the smallest subgroup (with respect to the \(\leq\) ordering) of \(G\) containing \(A\text{:}\) i.e., if \(H\leq G\) and \(A\subseteq H\text{,}\) then \(\angvec{A}\subseteq H\text{.}\) Using logical shorthand:
    \begin{equation} A\subseteq H\leq G\implies \angvec{A}\leq H\text{.}\tag{1.37} \end{equation}
