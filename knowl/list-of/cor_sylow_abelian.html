Corollary 1.185. Sylow theorems for abelian groups.

Let \(G\) be a finite abelian group of cardinality \(n> 1\text{,}\) and let \(p_1,p_2,\dots, p_r\) be the prime divisors of \(n\text{.}\)
  1. For each \(1\leq i\leq r\) there is a unique \(p_i\)-Sylow subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) \(N_i\text{,}\) and
    \begin{equation*} N_i=\{g\in G\mid \ord g=p_i^{n_i} \text{ for some } n_i\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
