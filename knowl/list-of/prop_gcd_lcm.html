Proposition 2.11.7. GCDs and LCMs.

Let \(R\) be a nonzero commutative ring, and let \(a,b\) be elements of \(R\text{.}\)
  1. An element \(d\) is a GCD of \(a\) and \(b\) if and only if \((d)\) is the smallest principal ideal of \(R\) containing \((a,b)\text{.}\)
