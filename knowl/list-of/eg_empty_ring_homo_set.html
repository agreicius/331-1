Example 2.5.5. \(\Hom(\Z/3\Z, \Z/2\Z)\).

Prove that \(\Hom(\Z/3\Z, \Z/2\Z)=\emptyset\)

Solution.
Assume by contradiction that \(\phi\colon \Z/3\Z\rightarrow \Z/2\Z\) is a ring homomorphism. Since in particular \(\phi\) is a group homomorphism, \(\phi([1]_3)\) is an element of \(\Z/2\Z\) of order dividing \(3\text{.}\) (See PropositionÂ 1.24.3.) But \([0]_2\) is the only such element. Thus \(\phi([1]_3)=[0]_2\text{:}\) a contradiction, since \([0]_2\ne 1\) in \(\Z/2\Z\text{.}\)
