Example 2.20.4. Aligned basis for \(\Z[i]\) as \(\Z\)-module.

Let \(R=\Z[i]\text{,}\) the ring of Gaussian integers, and let \(\alpha=1+2i\text{.}\)
  1. Show that \(M=\Z[i]\text{,}\) considered as a \(\Z\)-module, is free of rank 2.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Let \(N\) be the ideal of \(\Z[i]\) generated by \(\alpha\text{:}\) i.e., \(N=\alpha\Z[i]\text{.}\) Show that \(N\) is a free \(\Z\)-module of rank 2, and that \((1+2i, -2+i)\) is a \(\Z\)-basis of \(N\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Note that the standard \(\Z\)-basis \((1,i)\) of \(\Z[i]\) is not an aligned basis of \(M\) and \(N\text{.}\) Show that \((1+2i,i)\) is an aligned basis for \(M\) and \(N\text{.}\)
