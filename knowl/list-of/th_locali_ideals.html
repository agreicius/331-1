Theorem 2.10.18. Localizations and ideals.

Let \(S\) be a multiplicative subset of the nonzero commutative ring \(R\text{,}\) and let \(\lambda\colon R\rightarrow S^{-1}R\) be the localization map.

the ideal of \(S^{-1}R\) generated by the set \(\lambda(I)\text{.}\)

    \begin{align} S^{-1}I \amp = \{r/s\mid r\in I\}\tag{2.10.12} \end{align}

    \begin{align} S^{-1}I=S^{-1}R \amp \text{ if and only if } I\cap S\ne \emptyset\text{.}\tag{2.10.13} \end{align}
    Equivalently, \(S^{-1}I\) is a proper ideal of \(S^{-1}R\) if and only if \(I\) does not intersect \(S\) (i.e., \(I\cap S=\emptyset\)).
