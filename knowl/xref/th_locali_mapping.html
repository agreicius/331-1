Theorem 2.10.9. Mapping property of localizations.

Let \(S\) be a multiplicative subset of the nonzero commutative ring \(R\text{,}\) and let \(\lambda\colon R\rightarrow S^{-1}R\) be the localization map. Given any ring homomorphism \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow T\) satisfying \(\phi(S)\subseteq T^*\text{,}\) there exists a unique ring homomorphism \(\phi^*\colon S^{-1}R\rightarrow T\) satisfying
\begin{align*} \phi \amp =\phi^*\circ \lambda\text{.} \end{align*}
Moreover, \(\phi^*\) is defined as
\begin{align} \phi^*(r/s) \amp = \phi(r)\phi(s)^{-1}\text{.}\tag{2.10.7} \end{align}
