Proposition 1.191. Homomorphism sets and automorphisms.

  1. Homomorphisms from infinite cyclic groups.
    Let \(G=\angvec{g}\) be an infinite cyclic group and let \(H\) be any group. For any \(h\in H\) the map \(\phi(g^k)=h^k\) is a group homomorphism. As a result, the map
    \begin{align*} \Hom(G,H) \amp \rightarrow H\\ \phi \amp \mapsto h=\phi(g) \end{align*}
    is a bijection of sets. In particular, \(\Hom(\Z, G)\) is in bijective correspondence with elements of \(G\) via the map \(\phi\mapsto \phi(1)\in G\text{.}\)
