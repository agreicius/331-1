Proposition 2.5.25. Homomorphisms from simple rings.

Let \(R\) be a simple ring. If \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) is a ring homomorphism with \(S\) a nonzero ring, then \(\phi\) is injective.

Proof.

Since \(S\) is nonzero, the ideal \(\ker\phi\) is not equal to \(R\text{.}\) It follows that \(\ker\phi=(0)\text{,}\) and hence that \(\phi\) is injective (by group homomorphism properties).
