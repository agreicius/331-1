Theorem 1.76. Prime factorization formulas.

Let \(m\) and \(n\) be positive integers, and let
\begin{align*} m= \amp \prod_{i=1}^rp_i^{m_i} \amp n\amp=\prod_{i=1}^rp_i^{n_i}\text{,} \end{align*}
where \(\{p_1,p_2,\dots, p_r\}\) is a list of distinct prime integers, and \(m_i, n_i\in \Z_{\geq 0}\) for all \(1\leq i\leq r\text{.}\)
  1. We have
    \begin{align} \gcd(m,n) \amp =\prod_{i=1}^rp_i^{\min\{m_i,n_i\}} \amp \lcm(m,n)\amp=\prod_{i=1}^r p_i^{\max\{m_i, n_i\}}\text{.}\tag{1.32} \end{align}
