Theorem 2.81. Quotients of polynomial rings.

Let \(R\) be a nontrivial commutative ring, and let \(f\in R\) be a polynomial of degree \(n> 1\) whose leading coefficient is a unit in \(R\text{.}\) Additionally, let \(\pi\colon R[x]\rightarrow R[x]/(f)\) and write \(\pi(g)=\overline{g}\) for all \(g\in R[x]\text{,}\) so that in particular we have
\begin{align*} \overline{x}\amp = \pi(x)=x+(f)\text{.} \end{align*}
  1. The restriction \(\pi\vert_R\colon R\rightarrow R[x]/(f)\) is injective. Thus we can identify \(\pi(R)\) with \(R\text{,}\) and we will write \(\pi(r)=\overline{r}=r\) for all \(r\in R\text{.}\)
